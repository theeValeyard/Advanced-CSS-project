How to Recognize and Ease Your Pet's Anxiety

How to recognize and ease your pet’s anxiety?

Anxiety is more common in dogs than you might think. All dogs can get anxious at times, but if it is too frequent, you need to do something about it. There are a lot of factors that may result in your dog feeling anxious, and luckily there are a lot of things that you can do to help. The thing is, most people are not good at recognizing anxiety in their pets. And some breeds are more prone to anxiety than others. So, you need to train them differently.

If you need to leave your dog alone while you work, it is even more important. Your dog is your responsibility, and you need to take care of all its needs. A lot of the times a dog gets anxious when it thinks that it needs to protect you from any kind of potential danger. You need to make sure that you trust your dog at all times and your dog trusts you at all times. Being separated from you all day can lead to them being feeling alone, and they may wreck the entire house by the time you return. Now, it can also be because they are bored, but worry can also cause them to act like that.

Signs to look out for

  1. Does your dog whine a lot?
  2. Does your dog cower away in the presence of other dogs or humans?
  3. Does your dog get very shaky and restless?
  4. Does your dog not get along with other humans or dogs and prefer to be left alone?
  5. Does your dog show avoidance of certain objects or runs away in the presence of some things or people?
  6. Does your dog show aggression frequently? (Aggression is a symptom, not the cause of the problem, so remember to treat it like a symptom)

Do you trust your dog completely?

Lack of trust between the dog and the owner can lead to anxiety and abnormal behavior.

And most importantly, your anxiety has a lot to do with your dog being anxious. Dogs pick up your vibe. So, if you struggle with anxiety, it can be the major reason why your dog is too. Your pets copy your behavior, so you should do something for both your problems. If it gets too much, you might even need to give your pet anti-depressants to cope up with everything. These are the basic signs that your dog might be experiencing anxiety. But make sure that none of these things are a sign of a physical problem. Call a vet to make sure that everything is okay.

Here are a few things that can help make things better

  1. Practice obedience

    Practicing obedience with your dog can help things as when your dog is anxious and hears a familiar command, it helps it calm down. Start with basic things like sit, stand, stay, etc. Dogs can pick up these things fairly easily, keep them motivated with treats and you will achieve that in less than a week.

  2. Use positive reinforcement

    Use positive reinforcement to train your dog, which means, you reward it for every desirable action with a treat or something nice. Don’t punish them harshly when they don’t listen to you. They are animals, they need time to learn. Promote positive and friendly behavior.

  3. Exercise and playing

    Dogs need to train physically, they are made that way. Dogs can easily run miles as they have a really strong build. You want to keep them active physically. It is recommended that you go on at least three walks a day. Two of them can be small, like 15 to 20 minutes each. And one long, like 40 to 45 minutes of walking. Get them to physically exert themselves. If you need to wake up early to do that, so be it. If you have a tight schedule because of work, consider hiring someone to play with your dog. Remember, that you decided to have a dog, you need to take care of all its needs.

    You can also invest in some toys that allow your dogs to physically exert themselves and feel fulfilled at the end of the day. Work on having a really strong relationship with your pets.

  4. Don’t get them excited about leaving

    When you leave for work, don’t hug and kiss your dog goodbye. Especially if it is going to be alone for a long time. You are indirectly teaching your dog that this is the time where I leave you alone for a long amount of time. Instead, just leave without saying goodbye. Don’t talk to your dog before you leave.

    On your days off, practice that. Leave for 2 minutes, and then come back, then leave for a little longer, and come back. It teaches your dog that it needs to wait for you when you are gone and it is okay. And have a positive and non-stressful approach towards that.

  5. Give them something to do

    A lot of the times dogs get anxious when they don’t have anything to do. So, they sit for a minute, think ‘oh heck, I got nuffin to do’ and then wreck your entire house. Give them something to look forward to. Get them long-lasting chew toys or a ball thrower or something. Basically, something they can do instead of getting anxious.

  6. Make them feel comfortable inside and outside the house

    You want your dog to be comfortable and in control when someone comes to your house and not just lash out at them. It happens because your dog feels the need to protect you and just scares the hell out of the poor stranger in the process of doing so. And if you’re walking and your dog starts to act out if a stranger passes you by, it’s because of the same reason. You want your dog to be at ease when it is inside the house or outside. Make your dog comfortable with other people and just balance its instinct to protect you with how the world works. Make your dog understand that everything is not a threat.

Conclusion

It’s hard keeping a dog at times, but we all know it’s totally worth it. Invest some of your time and energy into it, and you can have a happy and fulfilled life for both you and your dog. Work on small things, and watch them bring big changes to your life. Give your dogs a direction. And if you feel like you’re not good enough for that, have someone train it for a while. Either way, your dog is your responsibility. We hope we were able to help out in some way. If you have any other suggestions, do let us know in the comments. We hope you have a good time with your pawfect woofs. Good luck!
