7 Reasons Why Some Art Is So Expensive

Art is much more than what meets the eye. The value of art has more to do with perception. Art has no boundaries, art has no rules, art doesn't dictate what art should be. But it is a billion-dollar industry at the same time. Being an artist comes with a lot of challenges, and appreciation of art is an art in itself. But it still triggers the mind, how something that looks like it's painted by a little child is worth millions of dollars. And what makes it more expensive than the next piece.

There are many angles to look at it, and if you're wondering how one can determine the actual value of art, or what makes it unbelievably expensive or worth nothing at all, this article is for you.

It's hard to put a value on art

Art is an independent discipline, so it's hard to put a value on art. However, there are some technical aspects that you can consider while evaluating it, like the size, how old is it, who made it, if it has been bought by someone before and the like. The value of art is determined by the perceiver. Something can be worth millions to a person and worth nothing at all to the next. If you connect to a piece of art, it is valuable for you, if you don't, it's simply not.

But that's not the way you should be looking at art. Unless you plan to buy a piece of art at a given time, you should never see it for it is worth economically. Art needs to be appreciated and admired. The market value of a piece of art keeps changing. In the end, the value of something is only going to be what people are willing to pay. And art should not be bound in terms of money.

Artist's perception of the art

Artists don't think of money when it comes to their craft. It's more of a reflection of themselves into the big wide world, and money is just a tiny aspect of it. Artists tell their story through art and to them the value of art lies in, how many people were able to connect to it. Art is not about beauty, it never was. It means different things to different people, its strength lies in the uniqueness, the raw passion, and the ability to convey those intense emotions. It will be beautiful to some, and it will be stupid to others. So, it depends on you if you were able to connect to something or not.

Artists often live a challenging life. It's not easy to sustain yourself being an artist. Because again, the value of something is only going to be what people are willing to pay for it at the end of the day. So, next time you look at a piece of art, make sure you encourage the artist. A tiny thing like that can make a huge difference to that person.

Simple curve of supply and demand

Whenever the demand for something increases, the supply has to catch up to satisfy the people. But when the supply is less, and the demand is immense, the thing becomes exclusive. The reason why some paintings are so expensive is because the artists who painted them are no longer alive anymore. The value of art increases significantly after the artist is dead. Because it makes that piece exclusive and more important. Most of the famous art by eminent artists are preserved in museums. That is why even if a painting looks like a toddler splashed paint on a canvas is worth millions sometimes.

Heavy cost of exhibitions and art galleries

It is not just the value of art that needs to be considered while evaluating it. Exhibitions and events are expensive. If you really want your art to be seen in the elite art galleries on the planet, that is going to cost you a buck. Being a member of an elite art club can be super expensive. So, even if you are someone who appreciates art, you won't be able to afford it. Art exhibitions, auctions, and other events cost money. To bring the art in front of people and making them aware of its existence is not cheap. So, it is another factor why some of the pieces of art are worth so much.

Rise of abstract art and modern art

The invention of camera technology led to a major shift in the art culture. Now, the camera could do a much better job at what art had been doing for years. So, art was no longer about representing the world with skill. The modern art was more focused on the abstract aspect. It was more about the feeling and representing yourself rather than the world. So, that was one of the huge shifts in the art culture and that definitely affected the way it is perceived by the world today.

Art is seen as an easy investment

Art is seen as an easy investment and wealthy people often use it for selfish means and not exactly for the purpose that it is meant for. The value of art keeps changing, and if you bought something for a million dollars today, the value of that piece would be much more than that in the next seven years. The elite class of the society uses art to avoid taxes and boost their image.

Think of it like this, you bought something for a million dollars and it is worth 10 million dollars after ten years, and you have an annual income of 20 million dollars. Now, you decide to donate your painting that is worth 10 million dollars, you only have to pay taxes for the remaining 10 million. And your huge charity will be reported and circulated around the world. On top of everything, your name will be mentioned beside the painting you donated where it is kept in a museum. So, it is a powerful way to manipulate and use art to your advantage.

It is not a particularly bad thing, but art should be seen as more than the dollar value of it. It should be seen for it was created for and what the artist wanted to convey, or what it means to you. Art is a tricky thing to judge, but those who live for it know, how wonderfully amazing it can be and how without it, the world will turn to grey.

Practicing art is expensive

Practicing art is not cheap. Artists often live hard lives. It's not easy to get by and local artists find it really hard to sell their stuff. The difference is almost groundbreaking. Either artists are crazy rich or barely getting by. But that's the beauty of it. Artists still stick to their craft even after being broke. The way they perceive reality, the way they see the world that hasn't been so kind to them, and the will to stand up and face it every single day is easier said than done.

Art is liberation, and it requires sacrifice and courage that very few possess. A struggling artist is a warrior within, fighting countless battles in the mind and the world alike. So, next time you see one, be thoughtful and compassionate. The life they choose to live is out on the edge, where it is beautiful and deadly at the same time. Like when the sun goes down, half in the shadows, half burned in flames.

In conclusion

All art is precious, all art is unique in its own way. Art is powerful and magnificent. No wonder some of it mad expensive. It lives to tell the tale of the artist. And that tale is what immortalizes the artist, well after his death. It is the beauty of it, that the reality may change, the moment or feeling captured will not. It is a sacred profession, it is the only thing that can truly set you free, so, let it.
