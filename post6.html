Taking Care of your Pet

Everyone wants a pet, but they don’t realize how much time, effort, and energy go into keeping them. It’s not as easy as it looks. So, if you are considering getting a pet, make sure you know what you’re getting yourself into. Otherwise, the poor animal will suffer and so will you. But having a pet is like having someone who helps you notice a rainbow in the darkest of days. So, they are worth going the extra mile for, that’s for sure. If you don’t have much experience with living with a pet, here are a few key things that you should keep in mind.

Vaccination

If you decide to keep an animal in your house with you, make sure it is safe for both of you. Consult your vet and complete your pet’s vaccination as recommended by your vet. Before your dog is completely vaccinated, don’t let it play or get in contact with other stray dogs or animals which may or may not be vaccinated. It keeps you and your dog at potential risk of catching something nasty.

Even after the vaccination is complete, make sure you clean everything after your dog comes in contact with anything that might infect it or you.

The environment

You want your pet to be in a clean and safe environment. At the end of the day, they are still animals, so you need to take care of their hygiene, food, safety, and everything. Your work will almost double when it comes to cleanliness. So, make sure you’re prepared for cleaning all the fur and mess. And you need to pay extra attention to make sure that none of you get sick. You can invest in some gadgets like a pet hair vacuum cleaner to make your job comparatively easier, but you have to know that hygiene is important. Remove everything that they can reach and harm themselves with, anything sharp, anything small. It’s almost like having a toddler, but this one almost never learns.

Grooming

Your pet needs grooming, and that’s a fact. Depending upon the breed and the kind of pet you have, these things will vary a little, but grooming your pet is important. Make sure that you give regular baths to your dog (you don’t need to bathe them too frequently, but consult your vet anyway), get their nails clipped from time to time (it will get painful for you when they try to play if their nails are too long and they might damage furniture and other things in the house). Don’t do it yourself if you don’t know how to do it. You can easily end up cutting the quick (also known as quicking). Basically, you will end up cutting too much and your dog will end up bleeding too much and it can take a long time for a nail quick to heal. Not to mention, it is extremely painful for your dog and they tend to remember it for a long time.

If you don’t know how to do it, just take it to your vet and he or she will do it for you. If your dog plays outside regularly, the nails will naturally grind to some extent. But if your pet is more like a house pet, you need to clip their nails regularly. And if your pet has a lot of furs, you will need to spend a lot on their hair products and haircuts too.

Food and Water

Providing good food is obviously a necessity. Your pet’s health depends a lot on its diet. And if you need to leave your pet alone for long durations, like when you go to work, make sure that it has access to clean drinking water and food. Invest in some good quality pet food and don’t forget to brush their teeth. Dental care is really important too. You can get them calcium chew toys to keep them busy and exercising their gums simultaneously. You can get a toothbrush that works like a chew toy, to keep your pet excited and healthy at the same time.

Play and exercise

Exercise and playing are really important for every animal, including humans. If your pet is more of a house pet, you need to give it even more attention. Exercise keeps your pets healthy and fit and it also gives you a reason to exercise with them. Don’t forget to take your pets on long walks and playdates. If you can’t do it yourself because of a busy schedule, hire someone to do it for you.

Regular veterinarian visits

You should visit your vet regularly to make sure everything is okay. Even if there is any kind of risk, it is better to know as early as possible. If you find anything unusual with your dog, call the vet to be sure.

Training and relationships

If you decide to keep a pet, and especially a big and aggressive pet, training is a must. You need to be able to communicate with your pet at all times. Having a strong relationship with your pet can make both of you feel fulfilled. If you don’t know how to train your dog or don’t have the time to train it yourself, hire someone to do it for you. Remember, at the end of the day, you want to be able to trust your pet completely, and your pet can trust you completely. Help your dog socialize with others, whenever you have time. You want your pet to be comfortable around other people as well.

Don’t let your pet roam unsupervised at all

Even if your dog is no longer a pup, you should still never let it roam unsupervised. At the end of the day, it is still an animal. It can get run over by a vehicle or get in contact with something toxic, maybe even eat it, and you won’t have the slightest idea. And it can scare other people as well, who don’t know it as well as you do. Even if your pet is friendly, there is always a chance of someone abducting it. You want to treat your pet like a toddler because honestly, that’s how smart they are. They can either be a really angry toddler or a really innocent one, either way, you don’t them unsupervised.
