When and How to Train your Dog

If you have a dog, training it properly should be one of the things you consider seriously. You can’t expect your dog to act right if it doesn’t know how to act right. In that case, you can’t blame it on the dog. And it may seem cute while he is a puppy, but once your dog is grown up, it can be really difficult to control him at times, as some dogs tend to grow very big. And it doesn’t even take that long. You won’t even realize where the time went.

Now, training your dog does not mean being too stern or uptight, it’s actually the exact opposite of that. We realize how cute and pawfect your furry paws are, but you also need to realize that you need to teach them certain things.

When can you train a dog

Many people believe that dogs can only be trained while they’re young. That’s absolutely not true. You can train a dog at any age. It’s just that, it is easier to control them while they are small.

You just need to have a strong bond with your dog. However, it is best that you start training a dog as early as you can. You can start when the puppy is about 10 to 12 weeks old. Start simple and then keep adding things to it.

Let the dog take his time

When you’re training your dog, your dog has some say in it as well. Take it easy, and let the dog take his time. Keep the dog motivated and make sure that he is having fun. Keep the experience rewarding. You can play with your dog to get him excited or give him his favorite treats when he does something right.

Make the dog feel safe and let him know that he can trust you, that you’re there and you won’t let anything bad happen to him. It will help him ease up be more comfortable.

If your dog is not comfortable with something, don’t force it. It is time to take a step back and let the dog adjust. Eventually, it will.

Play with your dog

Playtime is the most cherished time for our dogs. You can use it as a golden opportunity to train your dog. Use the toy of your dog’s choice and watch him circle around you like a Frisbee.

When you’re playing with your dog, his attention is focused on you. And at that time, he can learn and you can teach. So, use every opportunity you can.

Know when to stop

Dogs can be a little too excited at times, not knowing when to stop. So, it becomes your job. Be careful about how much you exercise your dog. Don’t overexert him. The younger the dog is, the more careful you need to be.

Also, younger dogs are really good at turning a deaf ear on you, so, you will really need to apply yourself to train them. They are not used to almost anything, so teaching them is quite a task. With dogs that are a little bit older, it is comparatively easier as they pick things up faster. Dogs mature as they age as most animals do.

How long should you train your dog

Well, it depends on a lot of things. But you should consider not doing very long sessions. An ideal session would be about 10 minutes. And you want to do it often. Have a lot of 10-minute sessions during the day, whenever possible. It is hard for dogs to keep their focus for long periods of time and you need them to be attentive for you to train them. It is always best to end a session before your dog gets frustrated.

It can be a lot for him to take, so be patient about it. It is going to take time, and in fairness, you will always have to teach him something. The process never really ends. The first thing you need to teach your dog is to teach him how to learn.

Should you hire a trainer for your dog

Well, it depends on your individual preference and situation. Is it possible to train your dog yourself? Yes, it is absolutely possible to do that, but it will take time and effort. There are tons of free resources on the internet that you can use. But if you can't do it because of your job, you should definitely invest in training your dog. If your dog is trained properly, he won’t damage the furniture in your absence and be comfortable around your guests.

So, if you can’t devote your time and energy into training your dog yourself, sign up for a training program.

Conclusion

You should at least give your dog some basic training, it is a necessity if you two are to co-exist. You should start as early as you can and take it easy on your dog, give him time to learn. He is an animal, his brain is not as big as yours. Make the experience rewarding and fulfilling for your dog. Don’t overexert him, you need to be extra careful if you’re training a young puppy. Divide the training into small fun bits and multiple times a day, instead of trying to train him for one hour straight. And make sure your dog is always comfortable around you. You should be able to trust your dog at all times and your dog should be able to trust you at all times. Consider hiring a trainer if you don’t have the time or ability to do it yourself.
