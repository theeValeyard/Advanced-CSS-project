Top 10 Must Visit Places in Scotland

Travel makes you feel good and alive, and it lifts up your spirits in the loneliest of times. And Scotland is considered to be the most beautiful country in the world, everything about this place is just magical. You need to take a trip to Scotland at least once in your lifetime. Trust me, you won't regret it. So, let's take a look at some of the must-visit places in Scotland.

Isle of the Skye

Isle of the Skye is one of the most perfect places on earth. It is surrounded by deep green valleys, lonely glens, mesmerizing skies, and romantic mountains. It makes you feel connected to nature and wildlife, and it has really gorgeous waterfalls. The edge of the world makes you feel so special and beautiful, that at that moment, you're actually free as a bird. The winds make you feel strong and weak at the same time, one of the precious moments when you feel courage and fear simultaneously.

If you ever go to the Skye, make sure you don't miss the fairy pools. They are located near the village of Carbost and they are a perfect place for photographers, walkers, and adventurous swimmers. You won't believe your eyes the first time you see them, it's something literally out of a fairytale.

Isle of Arran

Isle of Arran is like the miniature version of Scotland. It mirrors the entire Scotland in a very small place, and the best part is, you can take a ferry from Glasgow and reach there in less than an hour.

You should really consider staying for a few days to explore this sampler for the rich Scottish vibe. The food is really great and it has a really dramatic coastline that you can admire for hours. The walkways and feel of the place is almost intoxicating. Feeling good is really important for a fulfilled life, and travel makes you feel good. The scenery and the charm of Scotland will melt your heart.

The northern highlands

Every single place in Scotland is so picturesque and beautiful that you won't be able to decide which one is better. The way northern highlands change color through the seasons makes them look almost too good to be true. The wildlife, greenery and scenic mountains will keep you coming for more.

If you like trekking, it can be a really memorable trip for you and your loved ones. Check for the weather conditions though, weather can take a toll on you. It rains quite often, so if you're not prepared or not able to manage during the trek, you can get sick and it can be pretty difficult to handle the situation. Also, speaking of rain, you shouldn't cancel your plans due to rain or wait for it to stop, Scotland is famous for its changing weather and rain. You can still have a great time though, you just need a little more determination.

Edinburgh Castle

Scotland has way over 2000 castles, and a lot of them are beautiful pieces of architecture. But Edinburgh Castle definitely stands out. The skyline of Edinburgh isn't complete without the silhouette of the mighty castle.

The royal mile below stretches elegantly and you can still feel the charm of the capital city to this day. The castle hill looks like a crown and is one of the top paid tourist attractions of the city. And it is definitely worth checking out. Travel doesn't only takes you to different places, it also takes you back in time. Travel makes you feel good about all the things that you get to feel in life. It is a perfect example of investing in experiences and not materialistic things.

Kelvingrove Art Gallery and Museum, Glasgow

It is one of the most marvelous museums in Glasgow and it is built classic Glasgow style. A fire is known to have devastated it in the past, but it still holds a great cultural and historical value. There are exquisite pieces of art and craft waiting to be admired by you. It is the primary location for those associated with art. Yes, it is exactly how you are imagining it.

Glasgow is one of the best cities in the entire Europe because it gives the classic Hogwarts vibe. So, if you're a Harry Potter fan, you will fall in love with this place. Everything about the city is so charming that and Edinburgh and Glasgow are the hub of music and art.

So, if you're an artist, or if you like art, this is the place for you. The city fests, the dreamy vibe, and the energy of the place will blow your mind.

Monster of Loch Ness

The legend of Loch Ness or the Nessie made this place even better for a must-visit. Nessie was considered to be a vicious monster in the waters of Loch Ness which got international attention in 1993. There are many interesting stories and folk tales about the existence of it, but it's definitely something that you should experience first hand.

The location is really good and the water gives the entire landscape a mirror feel. Which makes it look and feel even more mystical and supernatural. You seriously don't want to miss out on this one.

Loch Lomond

Loch Lomond is Britain's largest lake and it's a short distance from Glasgow. It is a really beautiful place, and you should definitely visit it. The famous author Walter Scott calls it the "Queen of Scottish lakes".

It's a spectacular piece of nature's art and it will definitely leave you awe-stricken. If you go to Scotland with the love of your life, it can be a really memorable journey for the two of you. It's one of the most romantic places of all time.

Loch Lomond and a boat ride in the arms of your spouse would definitely be one of the cherished memories of your life.

Highland Folk Museum in Newtonmore

Newtonmore is Britain's first mainland open-air museum. It gives you the exact vibe of how people lived in the highlands in the older days. It gives you a good idea of what they ate, what they wore, how they lived and the related things. It's innovative and relaxing. There are no charges for entry into the museum and you won't regret it. Places like this make you feel how traveling is the best form of pilgrimage. Travel makes you feel good and pure, and it's a way to keep our childish curiosity that society forces us to give up on.

Stirling Castle

Stirling Castle is another one of Scotland's dazzling castles. If you love history, you will love this place. The views from the castle are mind-blowing and it gives you the 16th century vibe. Everything in the castle will take you back in time and it is one of the best preserved renaissance buildings in the UK.

Costumed interpreters interact with the visitors to give it an even more surreal feel. The royal history will leave your eyes wide open, the culture of the place is still felt. It is definitely one of the must visit places in Scotland.

Buachaille Etive Mòr in Glencoe

If you are a hiker or a rock climber, this place is the best fit for you. It gives the option to feel nature up close and it can prove to be one of the best treks of your life. Waterfalls and snow- peaked mountains can take your heart away to a different place.

It is like an adventure calling you to be a part of it, and it is an opportunity that shouldn't be taken lightly. The soul thirsts for it and your heart beats for it. It's one of those moments where you actually get to live in the moment. Buachaille Etive Mòr is definitely worth a visit.
