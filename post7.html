How to Take Care of a Newborn Puppy?

Dogs are considered man’s best friends, and for good reason. They complete your life in a way that nothing else can. So, let’s learn how to welcome these fur angels into our world and how to care for them in the first few weeks of their life. It can be intimidating at times, but soon enough, they will turn into playful bundles of joy and all your efforts will be worth it.

Taking care of any newborn animal is a responsible task and you want to be prepared for it. For the first week, they are most vulnerable and require the most attention and care. If the mother is present to take care of the puppy, it is the best case scenario and you should just leave it alone for the most part. She takes care of almost all the puppy's needs. You still need to take care of their hygiene and keep the mother and the puppies warm, but you won’t need to handle the puppies a lot. Make sure you provide a clean, safe, and warm environment and avoid any contact with any other animals. But if the mother is not well enough to take care of the pups, you need to nurse them yourself.

Taking care of a newborn puppy without a mother

The first week is the most critical. Newborn puppies need to be well fed and you need to nurse them every 2 hours. Don’t feed cow milk or regular milk to a newborn puppy, they can’t digest it and it might make them sick. Use a canine milk replacer to supplement and feed them every 2 hours. Use a syringe to feed them (you can later shift to a tube as they grow older). And make sure that all the paws are on the floor or your lap while feeding them, don’t feed them the way you would feed a human baby. It can result in filling their lungs and it can be really dangerous.

Take extra care of the temperature. Most of the young puppies and kittens don’t make it due to a lack of proper warmth. If you are using a heating pad that turns off on its own after a while, you will be putting them in potential danger if you don't check up on regular intervals. You need to make sure that they are warm at all times. Newborn puppies or kittens can’t self thermoregulate, that’s why heat is one of the major concerns. You can also use a rice sock to keep them warm, it retains heat for a long period of time. Also, make sure that if you’re using a heating pad, it should not be too hot. Newborn puppies need to be handled very gently.

Newborn puppies can’t use the bathroom on their own. The mother usually licks them to stimulate them to urinate or go potty. If the mother is not around, you need to do that. Use a soft tissue or a baby wipe to assist them in going to the bathroom every two hours.

Young puppies need a lot of affection, and in the absence of their mother, you should weigh them daily and make sure there is steady weight gain. Show affection to make sure that they are not stressed.

Newborn puppies need a lot of sleep, they will be sleeping for the most part, waking up only to eat or use the bathroom.

Consult a vet early on, especially if you are doing it for the first time. Make sure you are doing everything under proper medical guidance. If the puppies are healthy, you should wait for about 6 weeks before bringing them to the vet for a checkup. But if you feel like something is wrong, immediately call the vet.

Here are some of the things that you should be on a lookout for:

  1. Constant crying
  2. Pale gums
  3. Breathing difficulty
  4. Coughing/ wheezing
  5. Diarrhea
  6. Vomiting
  7. Poor weight gain
  8. Lack of apatite
  9. Any strange discharge from the mouth or eyes

If you notice anything like that, consult a doctor immediately. Newborn puppies are susceptible to disease, especially in the absence of the mother.

Consider consulting an animal shelter in your locality. You can find volunteers with experience in helping you take care of the puppy. And it significantly increases the chances of the puppy’s survival. They can also assist you to properly take care of the puppy.

Conclusion

Any newborn animal needs proper caring and you need to devote your day and night into doing that. The first few weeks are the hardest as the newborns are at their most vulnerable stage and require all your effort and attention. But that is a special experience in itself. Not to mention how ridiculously cute they are. However, you do not want to do something like this unprepared. Make sure you are properly equipped to handle any situation that arises. Their life is quite literally in your hands, so you need to be really careful.

I hope that you are able to save some lives.
